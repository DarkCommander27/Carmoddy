# Parts List - 1992 Ford Escort EV Conversion

## Required Nissan Leaf Components

### Primary Drivetrain Components

#### Electric Motor (EM57)
- **Part Number**: 2951A-3NA1A (varies by Leaf year)
- **Specifications**:
  - Type: Permanent Magnet Synchronous Motor
  - Power: 80 kW (107 hp)
  - Torque: 254 Nm (187 lb-ft)
  - Voltage: 360V nominal
  - Weight: ~130 lbs
- **Source**: Salvage Nissan Leaf (2011-2017)
- **Quantity**: 1
- **Estimated Cost**: $500-$1,500

#### Motor Controller/Inverter (PDM)
- **Part Number**: 292B0-3NA0A (varies by year)
- **Specifications**:
  - Power capacity: 80 kW continuous
  - Input voltage: 360V DC
  - Output: 3-phase AC
  - Cooling: Air-cooled
  - Weight: ~40 lbs
- **Source**: Same Nissan Leaf as motor
- **Quantity**: 1
- **Estimated Cost**: $400-$1,200

#### Battery Pack and Modules
- **Configuration**: 24 kWh or 30 kWh pack
- **Modules**: 48 modules (2 cells each)
- **Voltage**: 360V nominal (403V fully charged)
- **Capacity**: 
  - 24 kWh pack: 66.2 Ah
  - 30 kWh pack: 82 Ah
- **Weight**: ~650 lbs
- **Source**: Salvage Nissan Leaf
- **Quantity**: 1 complete pack or equivalent modules
- **Estimated Cost**: $2,000-$5,000 (depends on condition/capacity)

#### Battery Management System (BMS)
- **Part Number**: 295B0-3NA0B
- **Function**: Monitors cell voltages, temperatures, balancing
- **Source**: Included with Leaf battery pack
- **Quantity**: 1
- **Estimated Cost**: Included with battery pack

### Electrical Components from Leaf

#### On-Board Charger (OBC)
- **Part Number**: 292C0-3NA0A
- **Specifications**:
  - Input: 120V or 240V AC
  - Output: 360V DC
  - Power: 3.3 kW or 6.6 kW (depending on year)
  - Includes J1772 port connection
- **Source**: Nissan Leaf
- **Quantity**: 1
- **Estimated Cost**: $300-$800

#### DC-DC Converter
- **Part Number**: 292A0-3NA0A
- **Specifications**:
  - Input: 360V DC
  - Output: 12V DC
  - Power: ~100A continuous
  - Charges 12V auxiliary battery
- **Source**: Nissan Leaf
- **Quantity**: 1
- **Estimated Cost**: $200-$500

#### Contactors and Relays
- **Main Contactors**: High-voltage switching (from Leaf)
- **Pre-charge Resistor**: Soft-start circuit (from Leaf)
- **Service Disconnect**: Manual safety disconnect (from Leaf)
- **Source**: Nissan Leaf high-voltage junction box
- **Quantity**: As needed from donor vehicle
- **Estimated Cost**: $100-$300

#### Wiring Harness Components
- **High-voltage cables**: Orange-sheathed HV cables
- **Connectors**: Original Leaf HV connectors
- **Shielded cables**: For CAN bus communication
- **Source**: Nissan Leaf
- **Quantity**: Harvest from donor vehicle
- **Estimated Cost**: Minimal (harvest from donor)

### Important Notes

1. **Battery Health**: When buying used Leaf batteries, check capacity and cell balance
2. **Complete Donor**: Often cheaper to buy a complete wrecked Leaf than individual parts
3. **Compatibility**: Ensure all Leaf components are from compatible model years
4. **Testing**: Test all major components before installation
5. **Shipping**: HV batteries are expensive to ship; local pickup often better
